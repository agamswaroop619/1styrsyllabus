0

3

Objectives:

1: To introduce students to the principles and techniques of technical drawing and graphic representation. 2: To familiarize students with the conventions and standards used in engineering drawings

3: To provide understanding of orthographic projection, isometric drawing, and other standard drawing techniques used in engineering design.

4: To enable students to create accurate representations of complex 3D objects

5: To teach students the importance of effective communication through technical drawings in the engineering design and manufacturing processes.

Course Outcomes (CO):

At the end of the course, the student will be able to:

CO1: Interpret different types of engineering drawings, including orthographic projections, isometric views, and section views. CO2: Apply graphical conventions for dimensioning, scaling, and annotations in engineering drawings.

CO3: Understand geometric principles such as section, intersections and developments to solve real-world engineering problems.

CO4: Communicate ideas and designs effectively through clear, precise, and standardized technical drawings and 3D models. CO5: Apply the principles of technical drawing to real-world engineering problems in fields such as mechanical, civil, and

electrical engineering.

Unit No.

Topics to be Covered

Learning Outcomes


1.

GENERAL SCALES, LETTERING, VARIOUS TYPES OF PROJECTIONS:

Projection of Points and Lines: Elements of projection. Problems of points and lines. Trace True length, inclination and shortest distance.

Projections of Planes and Solids: Projection of plane figures. Traces of planes. Angle of Inclination of planes. Problems of points and planes, lines and planes. Angle between line and plane. Point of intersection. Intersection of planes. Dihedral angle. Projection of solids such as prism, pyramid, cylinder, cone,

sphere. Auxiliary views. Plane sections.

Understand the fundamental principles of projection used in engineering drawing, including the types of projection methods (e.g., orthographic, isometric). Detailed study related to the projection of point, line, plane, solid and further sections of solid.


2.

INTERSECTION AND DEVELOPMENT OF SURFACES: Intersection

of cylinders, cones, prisms, pyramids. Development of various surfaces including the interpenetrated and sectioned solids

Understanding of the concept of intersection between various types of solids, such as cylinders, cones, prisms, and pyramids. Learn the principles of development for various surfaces, such as conical, cylindrical, and pyramidal surfaces, including how to unwrap these 3D Surfaces into 2D.


3.

ISOMETRIC PROJECTION: Isometric scale. Projection of geometrical solids and various types of wood joints.

Understand the principles of isometric projection and isometric scale, where an object is represented in three dimensions on a two- dimensional surface, with equal foreshortening

along all three axes.


4.

PLANE GEOMETRY: Construction and drawing of curves such as Parabola, Ellipse, Hyperbola, Involute, Cycloid, & Helix.

Mathematical principles behind each type of curve (Parabola, Ellipse, Hyperbola, Involute, Cycloid, and Helix). Learn the properties and characteristics of these curves, including their equations, standard forms, and physical

significance.


5.

MACHINE DRAWING (THROUGH WORK-BOOK): First and third

angle projections. Orthographic views from the supplied blocks and isometric drawings (sketching only) missing lines and missing views. Views full in section.

Rules for dimensioning. Printing. Size and location of dimensioning. B.I.S. codes and conventions.

Understand the principles of first angle and third angle projection, including how objects are represented in relation to the projection plane in both methods.

Learn the rules for dimensioning objects accurately, ensuring that all necessary dimensions are provided for manufacturing and

assembly.

MANUFACTURING PROCESSES I

3

Objectives:

  1. Highlight the significance of manufacturing processes in everyday life and their impact on modern industries.

  2. Provide a comprehensive introduction to essential manufacturing techniques and principles.

  3. Facilitate in-depth understanding of conventional manufacturing methods, including casting, metal forming, welding, and machining.

  4. Develop skills to critically evaluate and select suitable manufacturing processes for specific components.

  5. Equip students with the foundational knowledge required to make informed decisions in manufacturing applications.

Course Outcomes (CO):

At the end of the course, the student will be able to:

CO1. The expertise to select the most suitable manufacturing processes for optimal product fabrication. CO2. The ability to recommend effective designs for casting, forming, welding, and machining processes.

CO3. The skill to streamline manufacturing processes, reducing costs and minimizing workforce requirements.

CO4. Proficiency in identifying and controlling key process parameters while mitigating potential manufacturing defects. CO5. An enriched technical understanding and broadened perspective on the manufacturing industry, empowering them to

contribute with talent and leadership.

Unit No.

Topics to be Covered

Learning Outcomes


1.

INTRODUCTION TO MANUFACTURING: Manufacturing

processes and their classification. Socio-economic role. Role of sustainability in manufacturing. Principles of Industrial Safety. Engineering Materials: Introduction, classification, properties, types and applications. Metallic materials (ferrous and non-ferrous metals & their alloys) and Non- metallic materials (Wood, ceramics & plastics). Elementary

introduction to heat treatment.

Explain the classification of manufacturing processes and assess their socio- economic impact, role in sustainability, and principles of industrial safety. (Understanding, Evaluating) Classify engineering materials and describe their properties, types, applications, and basic heat treatment processes. (Understanding, Applying)


2.

SOLIDIFICATION PROCESSES: Pattern: Materials, types, allowances, and color codes. Elements of the gating system. Moulding: Process, tools, sand, materials, classification of moulds, methods (Shell, Sodium silicate-CO2 and vacuum moulding). Machines. Cores. Melting furnaces and their operation. Casting: Expendable-mould processes (Sand, plaster, ceramic, rubber and expendable-graphite mould casting, lost-wax and lost-form processes), multi-use-mould processes (Gravity & pressure-die casting and centrifugal

casting). Casting defects

Identify materials, types, and allowances of patterns and explain the components and design of the gating system in moulding. (Remembering, Understanding) Analyse different casting processes (expendable and multi-use moulds) and identify common casting defects. (Analysing, Applying)


3.

DEFORMATION PROCESSES: Bulk Deformation Processes: Basic concepts of plastic deformation. Hot & cold working of metals. Theory and principle of common bulk deformation processes (Rolling, forging, extrusion, and drawing). Drop forging hammers (Mechanical, friction board and belt type) and Forging presses. Metal forming defects. Sheet Metal

Processes: Introduction.

Differentiate between hot and cold working of metals and explain the fundamental principles of bulk deformation processes like rolling, forging, extrusion, and drawing. (Understanding, Analysing) Examine metal-forming equipment, such as forging hammers and presses, and identify common metal-

forming defects. (Analysing, Understanding)


4.

MATERIAL-REMOVAL PROCESSES: Introduction to Material- removal processes, Cutting Tools, Machine Tools, Tool Wear & Coolants. Construction, specification, working principles and operations of following machine tools: Lathe, Drill, Milling, Shaper/Planer, and Grinder. Other Subtractive Manufacturing Techniques

Describe the principles, construction, and operations of machine tools (lathe, drill, milling, shaper/planer, and grinder), and classify cutting tools and coolants. (Understanding, Analysing) Analyse the causes of tool wear and suggest suitable coolants for specific material-removal operations to enhance efficiency.

(Analysing, Applying)


5.

JOINING AND ADDITIVE MANUFACTURING PROCESSES:

Joining Processes: Gas welding, Arc welding and Resistance welding processes, Fluxes, Filler materials, Welding defects, Introduction to Additive Manufacturing Processes.

Explain the principles, materials, and equipment involved in gas, arc, and resistance welding processes and identify typical welding defects. (Understanding, Evaluating) Describe the basics of additive manufacturing processes and compare them with traditional manufacturing techniques.

(Understanding, Analysing)

Objectives:

  1. Provide hands-on training in moulding, casting, and tool usage, enabling students to fabricate patterns and components precisely.

  2. Develop fundamental fitting skills by performing practical tasks such as filing, hacksawing, marking, and cutting on mild steel, emphasizing accuracy and craftsmanship.

  3. Familiarize students with carpentry tools, wood properties, and joint-making techniques, fostering expertise in constructing durable and precise wooden structures.

  4. Introduce and demonstrate welding techniques, including Gas, Arc, and Resistance welding, along with edge preparation and understanding of various weld types and joints.

  5. Cultivate an appreciation for sustainable practices by understanding artificial woods and adhesives and adequately handling

and maintaining workshop materials and tools.

Course Outcomes (CO):

At the end of the course, the student will be able to:

CO1: Develop various moulds from patterns such as Bevel Gears, Pulley, and File Handles, and gain practical experience in the casting process and moulding tools.

CO2: Utilize fitting tools and perform tasks such as filing, hacksawing, marking, cutting, and chipping to work with mild steel pieces, ensuring precision and accuracy in operations.

CO3: Identify different types of wood, properties, defects, and preservation methods, and construct joints like Cross Lap, Mortise & Tenon, and Tee-lap using carpentry tools.

CO4: Demonstrate the working principles of Gas, Arc, and Resistance welding processes, prepare edges, and understand types of welds and welding joints.

CO5: Understand the importance of artificial woods, adhesives, and proper handling of workshop tools and materials to

ensure sustainable and efficient practices.
